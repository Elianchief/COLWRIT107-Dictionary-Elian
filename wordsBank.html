All Words

AGÜEVO
[ah- weh-boh]
Sinónimo de afirmar un evento u opinión. También se refiere a alguien que dice la verdad.
English: Synonym of confirming or supporting an event or opinion. Also refers to someone saying the truth.
ALERO
[ah-leh-roh]
Dicho de un amigo o colega muy allegado. Es un colega de profesión o el chero que siempre está a tu lado, en las malas y las buenas. Proviene de ‘ala’ y equivale ‘mi mano derecha’.
English: Synonym of a very close friend or colleague. This colleague is professionally your “chero” (also means friend) who's by your side through thick and thin. Comes from ‘ala’ which is equal to ‘my right hand’.
AGÜITADO
[ah-gwee-tah-doh]
Sentir vergüenza, sentirse apenado o timido.
English: Feeling very ashamed, embarrassed, or just feeling/acting shy.
BICHO/A
[bee-choh]
Infante, niño. Es muy usada en los pueblos al igual que “Cipote”.
English: An infant. It’s used as often as “cipote”.
BOLADO
[boh-lah-doh]
Máxima expresión salvadoreña para referirse a algún objeto cuyo nombre no se sabe.
English: Ultimate salvadoran expression to refer to an object which we don’t know. Used to save time to refer to something instead of remembering the name of it.
BOLO
[boh-loh]
Ebrio, Adicto a bebidas alcohólicas.
English: Someone who is drunk. Someone addicted to alcohol.
CHUCO
[choo-koh]
Persona que tiene malos habitos higiénicos, algo asqueroso o sucio.
English: Someone who has bad hygiene. Also used to refer to something that is nasty or dirty.
CHELE
[cheh-leh]
Persona de tez blanca en demasía o pálida.
English: person with white or pale skin (To refer to white/blond people)
CHUCHO
[choo-choh]
Perro. También dícese a las personas tacañas.
Word: A dog. Also used to refer to someone who is greedy.
DESVERGUE
[Des-vir-geh]
Desorden. Situacion divertida fuera de control.
English: A situation in disorder. A fun situation that is out of control.
DÉMOLE
[Deh-mOh-leh]
Vamos, ponte en accion. Se traduce a "Vamos a hacerlo".
English: To put yourself in action. Translates to "Let's do it".
DENDIOY
[Den-dih-oi]
Hace unos instantes.
English: To say that something just happened or that happened a couple of minutes or hours ago.
EMBROCAR
[Em-bɾoh-kaɾ]
Voltear, dar vuelta a algo.
English: To turn something down.
ENCULADO
[En-cuh-ladoh]
Estar profundamente enamorado de alguien. Romantico o platonico.
English: Being deeply in love with someone. It could be romantic or platonic.
ENCACHIMBADO
[En-cah-shim-ba-doh]
Persona enojada, molesta o muy furiosa.
English: Someone who is mad, bothered, or very furious.
FERIA
[feh-ryah]
Dolar, dinero, o centro de atracciones.
English: A dollar, money, or an amusement park
FIERRO
[fyeh-rroh]
Un Machete o barra de metal. En ocasiones puede referirse también al pene del hombre.
English: A machete or an iron stick. In some instances it can also be used to refer to a penis.
FUFURUFA
[Fuh-fuh-ru-fah]
Mujer que es delicada, actitud que tiene de desprecio a los demás, con mucho ego y que todo le da asco. Generalmente una persona de mucho dinero y gustos refinados.
Word: A woman who is delicate and has an attitude of contempt for others. Has a high ego and most things disgust her. Genrally someone with a lot of money and refined tastes.
GUANCO
[Gwahn-goh]
Flojo, o demasiado extendido para su medida.
English: Something loose or too extended for a designed measurement.
GUAYABEAR
[Gwah-yah-be-ar]
Acción de un persona de aprender un conocimiento de algo, de forma poco profundizada, o mal hecha.
English: The action of someone who is learning something with not much depth or effort. Learning but in the wrong way.
HOCICO
[oh-see-koh]
Se refiere a la boca de alguien (Originalmente se refiere a la boca de un animal en espanol)
English: Someone's mouth (Originally it refers to an animals mouth in spanish)
HACEME BARRA
[ah-seh-meh] [bah-rrah]
Acompañar a una persona conocida a un lugar.
English: To go someowhere with a person you know.
ÍJOLE
[Ih-joh-le]
Expresión superlativa de sorpresa o disgusto.
English: Superlative expression of surprise or disgust.
INDIADA
[In-di-ah-da]
Expresión vulgar o de léxico "inferior" popular, que se dice proviene de orígenes precolombinos o mestizos
English: Vulgar expression to refer to something as “inferior” in popularity. It comes from pre-columbian or mestizos
JAINA
[Jaih-nah]
Novia o mujer en calidad de propiedad de un hombre.
English: A man's girlfriend (Or a women who "belongs" to a man).
JUELA
[Jueh-lah]
Expresar estar sorprendido por algo.
English: To express that you're surprised by something.
JURA

La policia
English: The police.

Fumar cigarrillo en la mañana..
English: To smoke cigarettes in the morning
LAMBIDO
[Lam-vih-doh]
Sin insumos económicos.
English: With a lack of money; "broke".
MAJE
[Mah-jeh]
Expresa amistad, modo para decir amigo/a.
English: Expression to refer to a friend.
MECHA CORTA

Persona que se enoja con facilidad.
English: A person who gets easily mad.
MAITRO
[May-troh]
Hombre mayor o anciano.
English: An elderly man (Or woman with; "maitra").
NAMBE
[Nahm-beh]
Rechazo o negación de algo, o desaprobación de algo previamente afirmado por otro.
English: Rejection or denial of something. Also the disapproval of something previously said by someone else.
NACO
[NAH-CO]
Adjetivo para una persona que llora fácilmente.
English: Adjective used to refer to someone who cries easily.
OSICO
[Oh-sih-co]
Boca de animal o persona.
English: An animal's or a person's mouth.
PASMADO
[Pahs-mah-doh]
Tonto, falto o lento de poder aprender algo.
English: To refer to someone who is "dumb", someone who's slow to learn something.
PAJA
[pah-hjah]
Mentira, locura.
English: A lie, something crazy.
PÚCHICA
[Puh-shi-kah]
Frase de desagrado ante cualquier situación.
English: Phrase to show displeasure in any situation.
QUIUBO / QUIUBOLE
[Kih-uh-bo] / [Kih-uh-bo-leh]
Expresión de saludo, para preguntar sobre algo.
English: Expression used as greeting (Used just as "What's up?").
RUCO
[Ruh-co]
Hombre mayor o anciano.
English: An older man or an elderly.
RÁFAGA
[rah-fa-gah]
Acción y efecto de estornudar o soltar GASES continuamente.
English: To sneeze or fart continuously.
SIMÓN
[Sih-mon]
Expresión de afirmación "callejera".
English: The "street" way of affirming or saying yes.
SE LA PICA
[Seh] [La] [Pih-ka]
Dícese de una persona que insinúa saber más que todos. Palabra utilizada por una persona que tiene envidia de otra.
English: Of a person who acts as if they know more than everyone. Used by people who envy others.
TALEGUIAR
[Tah-leh-gi-ar]
sinónimo de golpear.

TUFO
[Tuh-fo]
Mal olor, hediondez, pestilencia, gran patada.
English: A bad smell.
TROMPUDO
[Trom-puh-doh]
Persona que habla demás. Persona que se siente enojado. Persona que tiene labios grandes
English: Person who talks too much. Someone who is feeling angry. Or someone who has big lips.
UTUALITO

English: Saying that something just happened.
VERGUIAR

Dar una paliza o golpear fuertemente a otra persona.
English: Beat up or hit someone.
VIRGO

Persona que es muy vulgar.
English: A person who is vulgar.
WISTE

English: A piece of glass or broken glass.
WEBIAR
[Gweh-bih-ar]
Robar.
English: To steal something.
YINA
[Yih-nah]
Calzado común, tipo sandalia, utilizado generalmente dentro de las viviendas y lugares de recreación.
English: Common footwear such as flip flops. Generally worn inside the house or recreational places.
YUCA
[Yuh-cah]
Situación difícil.
English: A hard situation.
YUNAIS
[Yu-nah-is]
Los Estados Unidos.
English: The United States.
ZORRO/A
[Zso-roh]
Término derogatorio a hombre/mujer que se acuesta con muchas personas.
English: Derogatory term to refer to a man/woman who sleeps with a lot of people.
ZUMBA
[Zsum-bah]
Sinónimo de borrachera, pero en un grado más alto, el cual indica que se ingiere alcohol todos los días.
English: Synonym of drunkenness but in a higher way, meaning that the person has been drinking all day.
